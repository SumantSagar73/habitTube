HabitTube · User Manual · v1.0

User Manual

Everything you need to track habits, build cascading goals, run deep-work sessions, and understand your progress — all in one place.

Introduction

HabitTube is a personal productivity system built around three ideas working together: daily habits that build consistency, goals that cascade from yearly ambitions down to weekly actions, and focused work sessions that give your intentions real time behind them.

Unlike most habit trackers that treat habits and goals as separate tools, HabitTube links them — you can attach a habit to a goal, see how your completion rate drives goal progress, and review the full picture from a single Insights dashboard.

Habits
Daily, weekday, or custom-schedule habits with streaks, completion heatmaps, and mood tracking.
Goal cascade
Year → Quarter → Month → Week goals that roll up automatically. See how small wins build big outcomes.
Focus timer
Pomodoro-style sessions (10 / 25 / 50 / 90 min) linked to goals. Every minute is logged to Insights.
AI Coach
Chat with an AI that knows your habits and goals. It can propose a full plan — you approve before anything changes.
Calendar
Monthly overview showing habit completion, mood scores, and tasks for any day at a glance.
Insights
Year heatmap, best-day analysis, habit correlation, mood overlay, and goal health indicators.

Core philosophy

HabitTube is built on one premise: big outcomes are the sum of small daily actions. The system is designed to make that connection visible and measurable.

The cascade principle

Every goal in HabitTube lives at one of four levels: Year, Quarter, Month, or Week. Goals at lower levels link to parents above them. The system automatically rolls up progress — if all your week goals are done, your month goal inches forward; if all months close out, the quarter goal moves.

You always see where you are in the chain. A weekly task connects to a week goal, which feeds a month goal, which builds toward a quarter goal, which advances your year ambition.

Offline-first, your data

All data is saved to your browser's localStorage instantly. Supabase sync happens in the background (1.2 s debounce after every change) and uses a last-write-wins strategy — the newer timestamp always wins. If you go offline, you keep working. When you reconnect, changes sync automatically.

Note
Because data is tied to your browser storage, clearing browser data will erase local state. Use Settings → Backup regularly to export a JSON snapshot you can restore later.

Quick start

You can be up and running in under five minutes. Here is the recommended first-run path:

1
Sign inOpen HabitTube. Sign in or create an account — this ties your data to Supabase so it syncs across devices.
2
Create a habitClick + New → Habit. Give it a name, an emoji, and pick which days it should appear. Start with one habit — don't over-commit on day one.
3
Set a year goalClick + New → Goal or go to the Plan tab. Set a big outcome for the year. Keep it one sentence.
4
Break it downIn Plan, drill into the current quarter and add a quarter goal that feeds your year goal. Then do the same for this month.
5
Check off todayGo to Today and mark your habit done. Start a focus timer on something that matters. You're in the system.
Tip
Not sure what habits to build? Click Habits → Library to browse four starter packs (Morning Routine, Fitness, Learning, Mindfulness) and install any with one click.

Today

The Today view is your daily command center. It shows everything due right now: habits scheduled for today, your focus timer, tasks for today's date, your mood check-in, and a journal entry field.

Habit checklist

Only habits scheduled for today's day of the week appear here. Tap a habit row to mark it done — the checkbox fills and the streak counter increments. Tap again to un-mark it.

Habits with a set time show a small clock label. If the time has passed and the habit is undone, it shows an overdue tag.

When you complete every habit due today, a confetti animation fires automatically. You have earned it.

Streak leaders

The right column shows the top 5 habits sorted by current streak length, with their all-time best streak alongside. This is a quick reminder of what you have built and what is at risk.

Focus timer

The focus timer lives in the Today view. Pick a session duration (10, 25, 50, or 90 minutes), optionally type what you are focusing on and link it to a goal, then press Start session.

Press the fullscreen icon (top-right of the timer card) to enter distraction-free mode — a full-screen countdown with nothing else on screen except the HabitTube wordmark and session controls.

When a session ends, it is automatically logged to focusLog with the date, duration, goal link, and label. You can see all past sessions in Insights → Focus.

Important
The focus timer state is not persisted to storage — if you refresh the page mid-session, the timer resets. Keep the tab open during a session.

Completed sessions appear as small dots in the timer card and the fullscreen view. Each dot represents one session logged today.

Daily tasks

Daily tasks are to-dos scoped to a specific date. They appear in the Today view for today's date only. You can:

  • Add a task with a priority (low / medium / high) and an optional goal or habit link.
  • Click a task title to edit it in place. Press Enter or click away to save.
  • Toggle the checkbox to mark done / undone.
  • Use the → arrow to move a task to tomorrow.
  • Delete with the × button.

Recurring tasks

Recurring tasks auto-generate on a schedule. Open the Recurring panel (collapsible at the bottom of the task list), fill in a title and repeat pattern (daily / weekdays / weekly), and save. From then on, the task appears automatically each applicable day.

Mood & journal

At the bottom of the Today view you can log a mood score (1–5, from Rough to Great) and write a free-form journal entry. Both are stored keyed by date and appear in the Calendar view.

Mood data feeds the Mood & habits chart in Insights, which shows whether your habit completion rate and mood scores move together over time.

If you miss a habit, you can also record a miss note explaining why — useful for spotting patterns in what causes you to fall off track.

Plan

The Plan tab is where you build and track your goal hierarchy. It has two modes toggled in the top-right: Cascade and Tree.

Cascade mode

Cascade mode is the main planning view. It shows one level of the hierarchy at a time (Year, Quarter, Month, or Week) and lets you drill down.

The cascade ribbon

At the top of the view is a connected ribbon of pill buttons: 

[2026  ████░ 72%]  ›  [Q2  ████░ 58%]  ›  [Jun  ███░░ 41%]  ›  [W26  ██░░░ 35%]

Each pill shows the period label, a mini progress bar, and the completion percentage. The active (deepest) level is highlighted. Click any pill to jump back up to that level. Use the ‹ › arrows on the left to step backward and forward through time (e.g. previous quarter, next month).

Left sidebar

The sidebar shows:

  • Period progress card — current period label, overall % as a large number, and a progress bar. At year level, a text area lets you record your north-star vision for the year.
  • Child period cards — Q1–Q4 when at year level, months at quarter level, weeks at month level. Each card shows a health indicator dot (green = on track, amber = slightly behind, red = at risk) based on how far through the period you are vs how much is completed.
  • Carry-forward — incomplete goals from the previous period appear here with a one-click button to bring them into the current period.

Right column — goals

The right side shows goals for the current level with a Quick Add input (type and press Enter) and an + Advanced Goal button that opens the full goal editor.

Goal types

Every goal has a type that determines how its progress is calculated:

TypeProgress logicUse when
Checklist Done / not done. If it has sub-goals or linked tasks, rolls up from their average. Otherwise a manual "Mark done" button appears. Binary outcome — either you shipped the feature or you didn't.
Numeric current / target × 100. Use the − / + buttons on the card to increment, or type a value directly. Measurable quantities — km run, books read, revenue.
Habit Counts completions of a linked habit up to a target number. "Complete this habit 20 times this month."

Any goal type can also be manually overridden — click the pencil icon on a goal card and type a percentage directly. A "manual · reset to auto" button appears to remove the override.

Goals can have a parent goal at the level above. When a goal has sub-goals, its progress is the average of their percentages rather than its own direct value.

Week task board

When you drill down to a Week, a 7-day task board appears below the goal cards. Each day is a card with its tasks listed. You can:

  • Add tasks per day with priority and goal/habit link.
  • Click a task title to rename it in place.
  • Move tasks to the next day with the arrow button.
  • Link tasks to any goal in the current week's ancestor chain.

Today's column has a bold border so it stands out from the rest of the week.

Weekly reviews & carry-forward

If last week had activity and you haven't reviewed it yet, a Your weekly review is ready banner appears at the top of the Plan tab. Click it to open the review modal.

The review modal lets you:

  • Rate each goal's effort (1–5 stars).
  • Write a reflection note.
  • Carry incomplete tasks forward to the current week with one click.
  • Mark the review done to clear the nudge.

The AI Coach (if enabled) can generate a written reflection based on your week's data — click Generate with AI inside the review modal.

Tip
Do your weekly review on Sunday evening or Monday morning. It takes about 5 minutes and dramatically improves the quality of your next week's planning.

Tree mode

Switch to Tree mode (top-right toggle) to see all goals across all years and levels in a collapsible tree. Useful for auditing the full structure, editing goal relationships, or getting a bird's-eye view without time navigation.

Habits

The Habits tab (also called Manage) shows all your habits. Switch between List view (drag to reorder, click to view heatmap) and Grid view (tap cells to retroactively complete habits across days).

Creating a habit

Click + New → Habit or the + Add habit button in the Habits tab. The habit editor lets you set:

FieldDescription
NameThe habit's display name. Keep it short and action-oriented ("Read 20 min", "Walk").
EmojiVisual identifier shown in the checklist and calendar.
DaysWhich days of the week the habit appears. Default: every day. Common: Mon–Fri for work habits.
TimeOptional reminder time. Shows an overdue label if past this time and habit is undone.
Goal linkLink this habit to a goal so completions count toward goal progress.

To edit a habit, click its name in the Habits tab. To delete, open the editor and click Delete habit at the bottom.

Reordering

In List view, grab the grip handle (⠿) on the left of any habit row and drag it to a new position. The order is saved immediately.

Habit detail & heatmap

Click a habit's name in List view to open the detail panel showing: current streak, best streak, 30-day rate, and a personal full-year heatmap. Each square in the heatmap is one day — darker means the habit was done, empty means it was missed or not scheduled.

Recurring tasks

Recurring tasks are templates that generate a daily task automatically. They live separately from habits — a recurring task is a to-do that repeats, not a behavior you are building a streak for.

Good use cases: "Send weekly status update" (weekly), "Review inbox" (weekdays), "Water plants" (weekly).

To create one, scroll to the bottom of the Today task list and expand the Recurring section. Fill in a title and repeat pattern and press Add.

Habit library (templates)

Click the Library button in the Habits tab toolbar to browse four pre-built habit packs:

PackHabits included
Morning RoutineWake up early, make bed, meditate, exercise, read, cold shower
FitnessWorkout, walk 10,000 steps, stretch, track calories, drink 2L water
LearningRead 30 min, study/practice, journal, no phone first hour, podcast
MindfulnessMorning meditation, gratitude journal, screen-free evening, deep breathing, walk in nature

Installing a pack skips any habits you already have with the same name (case-insensitive). A pack shows "✓ Installed" once all its habits are present. You can install packs partially — just click individual habits from the pack list.

Insights

The Insights tab is a read-only analytics dashboard split into four sub-tabs: Overview, Habits, Goals, and Focus.

Overview

The Overview sub-tab has two sections:

Scoreboard

Lists all active habits for the current period with their completion rates as horizontal bars. Habits are sorted by completion rate descending — your strongest habits are at the top. A bar reaching 100% means you completed that habit every scheduled day this period.

Year at a glance (heatmap)

A full-year contribution graph. Each square is one day. The shade represents the fraction of scheduled habits completed that day — darker = higher completion rate. Hover any square to see the exact date and rate. Squares before your account was created are shown empty.

This view makes streaks, regressions, and seasonal patterns immediately visible. A long horizontal run of dark squares is your best motivator.

Habits analytics

Three analytical widgets appear in the Habits sub-tab:

Best day of the week

A bar chart showing your average habit completion rate per weekday over the last 90 days. If Sunday shows 78% and Wednesday shows 41%, you know your mid-week environment needs attention.

Habit pairs (correlation)

Shows which pairs of habits most often get done together (co-occurrence rate over 60 days, minimum 5 shared scheduled days). A high pair rate like "Meditate + Read: 89%" means doing one strongly predicts doing the other — they may be part of the same routine.

Mood & habits overlay

A 30-day area chart overlaying habit completion rate (solid line) and mood score (dashed line, scaled to 0–100). Only visible if you have logged at least one mood. Look for days when both lines drop — those are days worth examining in your journal.

Goal progress

The Goals sub-tab shows cards for every goal in the current Year / Quarter / Month / Week, filterable by level. Each card shows:

  • Goal type icon, title, level badge, period label, and parent goal reference.
  • Current progress percentage and a colored progress bar.
  • For numeric goals: current value vs target (e.g. "32 / 50 km").
  • An amber warning badge if the goal ends within 7 days and is less than 80% complete.

Focus history

The Focus sub-tab shows a log of all completed focus sessions, most recent first. Each entry shows the date, duration, session label, and linked goal. Total minutes for each day are summed.

Note
Only completed sessions (timer reached zero or you pressed Stop after at least some time) are logged. Paused sessions that you abandon are not recorded.

Calendar

The Calendar tab shows a month grid. Each day cell contains:

  • A shade fill representing habit completion rate for that day.
  • A mood face emoji if you logged a mood.
  • A count of tasks (done / total).

Click any day to expand it and see the full task list, journal entry, and miss notes for that date. Navigate months with the ‹ › arrows or click the month/year label to jump to today.

AI Coach

The Coach is a floating chat button (bottom-right of the screen). Clicking it opens a full-screen conversation panel. The AI has read-only access to your habits and goals — it can reference them in its responses.

Important
AI features require the dev server (npm run dev). They do not work in production builds because the Groq API key is injected server-side by the Vite proxy middleware. If you see "Groq key not configured", check that GROQ_API_KEY is set in your .env.local file.

Chatting with the coach

Type naturally. Example prompts that work well:

  • "I want to run a marathon in 6 months. Help me build a plan."
  • "My habit completion is low on Wednesdays. What should I change?"
  • "Create a Q3 plan for learning Python."
  • "Review my current goals and tell me if they're realistic."

Accepting a coach plan

When the coach proposes new goals or habits, it embeds a JSON plan in its reply. HabitTube strips the JSON out of the displayed message and renders it as a confirm / reject UI. You see exactly what will be created before anything changes. Click Apply plan to add the goals/habits, or Reject to discard them and keep chatting.

Changing the AI model

Go to Settings → AI. The default model is llama-3.3-70b-versatile. You can switch to a faster or more capable Groq model from the dropdown. Your API key can be tested with the Test connection button.

Settings & Sync

Open Settings via the gear icon in the top-right header.

Theme

Three options in the Appearance section: Light, Dark, Auto. Auto follows your operating system's dark/light preference and updates live if you change it.

Sync status

A small dot in the header shows sync state:

IndicatorMeaning
Green dot · SyncedLast write was successfully saved to Supabase.
Amber pulsing · SyncingA write is in progress (debounce window active).
Gray dot · OfflineNo network or Supabase unreachable. Data is safe in localStorage.

Export to Excel

Click Export in the header (or Settings → Export). This generates an .xlsx file containing your habits, completions, goals, and focus log as separate sheets. Useful for further analysis in Excel or Google Sheets.

Backup & restore

In Settings, Download backup saves the full app state as a .json file. Restore from backup loads that file and overwrites the current state. Always download a fresh backup before restoring, in case you need to go back.

Warning
Restoring a backup replaces all current data including habits, goals, completions, and focus history. There is no undo. Download a backup first.

Install as app (PWA)

If your browser supports PWA installation, a banner appears at the bottom of the screen offering to install HabitTube as a standalone app. Once installed, it launches without browser chrome and works offline. You can dismiss the banner permanently if you prefer to use it in the browser.

Reference — Goal cascade & period keys

Period keys are the strings HabitTube uses internally to identify time windows. Understanding them helps when reviewing exports or connecting habits to goals.

LevelFormatExampleMeaning
Year"YYYY""2026"Full calendar year
Quarter"YYYY-QN""2026-Q2"Apr–Jun 2026
Month"YYYY-MM""2026-06"June 2026
Week"YYYY-WNN""2026-W26"ISO week 26 of 2026 (Mon–Sun)

Weeks follow ISO 8601 — Monday is the first day of the week. Week 1 is the week containing the first Thursday of the year. A week belongs to the month/quarter of its Thursday (not Monday), which avoids weeks straddling year boundaries being misattributed.

Rollup rules

  • If a goal has child goals: progress = average of children's percentages.
  • If a goal has no children: progress comes from its own type logic (checklist done/not done, numeric current/target, habit completion count).
  • A manual override always wins regardless of children — the % you type is the % shown.

Reference — Streak rules

TermDefinition
Current streak Consecutive scheduled days the habit was completed, counting backward from today (or yesterday if today isn't done yet). Non-scheduled days are skipped and do not break a streak.
Best streak The longest consecutive run ever achieved for this habit.
30-day rate Completions ÷ scheduled days in the last 30 calendar days, as a percentage.
Streak reset Occurs when a scheduled day passes without a completion. The streak resets to 0 and starts rebuilding from the next completed day.
Tip
Streaks reset at midnight in your local time zone (the app uses your browser's clock). If you complete a habit after midnight, it counts for the new day — mark the previous day manually in the Habits grid view if needed.

Reference — Data & privacy

Where your data lives

HabitTube stores data in two places simultaneously:

  1. Browser localStorage — key habittube-data-v1. Instant, works offline. Cleared if you clear site data in your browser.
  2. Supabase — the whole state blob is stored as a single row in the states table, keyed by your user ID. Synced on every change (1.2 s debounce).

What is stored

A single JSON blob containing: habits array, completions map, goals array, tasks map, focusLog array, moods map, notes map, visions map, reviews map, recurringTasks array, chat messages, and settings (theme, AI model).

Cross-device sync

Sign in to the same account on a second device. On load, the app pulls from Supabase and uses whichever version has the newer timestamp — your most recent edit on any device wins.

Deleting your data

Clear localStorage in your browser dev tools (Application → Local storage → Delete key habittube-data-v1). To delete the Supabase record, contact the account admin or delete your Supabase account row directly.
